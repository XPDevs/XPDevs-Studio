Production Notes

Development

The Daughter of War started as a simple question: what if a woman in the late 1700s chose to fight not for a country, but for something personal? Not for a cause. For revenge. We spent a lot of time on research. Military history, period clothing, how women lived back then. If the details are wrong, the story falls apart.

The script went through a few rounds. We kept coming back to the same thing: this is not a war movie. The war is just where it happens. What matters is what she becomes.

Visual Approach

We went with muted colors, like old oil paintings. Lots of candlelight and grey skies. The landscape looks the way she feels, raw and cold.
